01

One resident is carrying the whole building's accounts.

The honorary treasurer is a working professional who took the job as a favour. There is no accountant, no software, no handover file — just their memory and goodwill. Today's "system" is three things stitched together, and each one fails in a predictable way.

  • WhatsApp group

    "Please pay maintenance" gets buried under 200 forwards. Nobody knows who actually paid; screenshots of transfers scroll away by Tuesday.

  • A diary & a shoebox

    Cash receipts, plumber bills and the electricity challan live in a physical box. Half are faded, one is missing, and the maths is done by hand.

  • One person's memory

    Only the treasurer knows who owes two months and who overpaid. When they hand over at the AGM, that knowledge walks out with them.

The books don't just get messy — they die at every annual handover.

02

The wedge

You don't need Tally and a chartered accountant for 45 flats. You need dues, receipts, and one clean report the next treasurer can actually inherit.

SamitiBook is deliberately small: the exact accounting a <50-flat society runs on, and nothing it doesn't. Clean enough that the handover is a login, not a shoebox.

Do I need to know accounting?

No. If you can fill an Excel row, you can run SamitiBook. Invoices come from the charge heads you set once, and the AGM report builds itself.

What happens when the treasurer changes?

The handover becomes a login. Every invoice, receipt and the running balance stay in SamitiBook — the new treasurer picks up exactly where the last one left off.

Does it actually send WhatsApp messages?

SamitiBook drafts the reminder and drops it into your outbox, ready to send from your own number. You stay in control of what goes to residents.
